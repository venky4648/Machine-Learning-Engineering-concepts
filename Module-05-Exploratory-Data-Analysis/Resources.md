# 📚 Resources & Cheat Sheets

> A collection of official documentation, cheat sheets, and highly recommended tutorials to help you master Exploratory Data Analysis.

---

## 🐼 Pandas Resources

1. **[Official Pandas Documentation](https://pandas.pydata.org/docs/)**
   - The absolute source of truth for syntax and parameters.
2. **[Pandas Cheat Sheet (PDF)](https://pandas.pydata.org/Pandas_Cheat_Sheet.pdf)**
   - Print this out! It contains visual guides for `groupby`, filtering, and aggregation.
3. **[Kaggle: Pandas Micro-Course](https://www.kaggle.com/learn/pandas)**
   - A fantastic, free, interactive 4-hour course on Pandas manipulation.

---

## 📊 Visualization Resources

1. **[Seaborn Official Gallery](https://seaborn.pydata.org/examples/index.html)**
   - Don't memorize Seaborn syntax. Look at this gallery, find the chart you want to build, and copy the boilerplate code.
2. **[Python Graph Gallery](https://python-graph-gallery.com/)**
   - A massive collection of hundreds of charts with the exact reproducible Python code.

---

## 🧠 Statistics for EDA

1. **[StatQuest with Josh Starmer (YouTube)](https://www.youtube.com/user/joshstarmer)**
   - The best channel on the internet for understanding statistics. Watch his videos on Normal Distributions, Variance, and Correlation.
2. **[Practical Statistics for Data Scientists (Book)](https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/practical-statistics-for/9781491952955/)**
   - Highly recommended reading. It explains which statistical concepts are actually used in industry and which ones you can ignore.

---

## ⚡ Automated EDA Tools (Advanced)

Once you master manual EDA, you can use these libraries in the industry to generate 50-page HTML reports in two lines of code:

1. **[YData Profiling (formerly Pandas Profiling)](https://github.com/ydataai/ydata-profiling)**
   - Automatically generates histograms, correlation matrices, and missing value reports.
   - `pip install ydata-profiling`
2. **[Sweetviz](https://github.com/fbdesignpro/sweetviz)**
   - Excellent for comparing Train vs Test datasets side-by-side visually.
